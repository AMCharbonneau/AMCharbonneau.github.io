Can I Get Therapy Online?

April 1, 2015

With advances in connectivity and media, there are many more options for seeking out therapy, including therapy over the telephone or online. This is a short guide to help you find quality help online.

Why Showing Up In Person Can Sometimes Be Better

First, when you take time out of your schedule to show up someplace, it requires a fairly serious commitment and can be a signal to yourself that you are prepared, willing, and ready to make some serious changes in your life. Second, we are human. We are used to looking at each others’ faces in person and making a connection. Sometimes important social cues and information can be lost over the telephone or over a bad internet connection. Third, having a local therapist will likely mean having someone who knows local resources better than someone who is located far away.

Why Therapy Online Can Sometimes Be Better

On the flip side, there can be some valid reasons to seek out alternative ways to participate in therapy. Seeing a therapist online can sometimes be much easier in terms of scheduling around your other commitments and for people who live in rural areas who would have to drive an hour or more each way to reach the therapist. Online or telephone therapy can also be extremely helpful for people who are home-bound by medical conditions such as cancer treatments, chronic pain, or other debilitating conditions. This type of therapy can also be a good way for people with more severe problems, such as trouble leaving the house or social phobia, to gradually start on the path to healing.

Whether you participate in person, over the phone, or over the internet, making the commitment to set aside uninterrupted time for therapy is key to getting the most of your therapy experience.

Tips for Finding a Therapist Who Will Do Online Therapy

Where Can I Find a Therapist Who Can See Me Online?

Conduct a google search for licensed therapists in who are practicing in your state or province. Good search terms are “licensed clinical psychologist” “licensed marriage and family therapist” and “licensed therapist” plus your location. If you are in a rural location, use a search term that includes the nearest major city, like “Seattle area”.

Finding a therapist who is licensed in your state or province provides you with protections in case something goes wrong. In the United States, the practice of therapy is still regulated separately by licensing boards in each state. The rules on getting therapy online vary state by state and usually depend on the location of the person seeking therapy. For example, California law states that a therapist seeing a person who is located in California must have a license to practice therapy in California.

Finding the Right Therapy Online

In the past few years, there have been several new companies popping up that are claiming to do online therapy, email therapy, or online counseling. I strongly encourage everyone to research these services carefully. When you are doing your research, think carefully about therapists’ training, privacy and confidentiality online, and payment (including whether your insurance will cover your sessions, if needed).

The most important thing to to consider is the training and licensure of the online therapists in the company. Think about your emotional health like your teeth: do you want someone with a few hours of training poking around in there? Or would you rather have someone with formal training, documented experience, and licensure to conduct their practice? If you wouldn’t see an unlicensed dentist, you probably don’t want to see an unlicensed therapist either.

Some Options for Online Services: Buyer Beware

A popular site in Europe, 7 cups of tea, connects people with “trained active listeners”. If you just need to vent to someone, maybe this could be a helpful service. If you are having serious issues in your life, your relationships, or your health, at best, this service could provide someone to listen to you, but not much more than that. At worst, someone with poor training and no licensing could potentially provide you with unhelpful or bad advice. In a review of their site in 2015, I also found no statement about privacy.

Another site operating in the US, breakthrough.com, seems to be employing online licensed therapists, psychologists, and psychiatrists, can bill some insurance companies, makes an effort to match people with licensed providers state by state, and has a special section on securing privacy.

TL;DR

There are many options for type of therapy and level of training of therapist. You can also find quite a few independent therapists, psychologists, and nurse practioners who are willing to conduct therapy online.

Ultimately, it’s up to you to decide what is the best match for you.
